The Ripcord Manifesto

"We started Ripcord because we were tired of being the product. Tired of platforms that prioritize advertisers over users. Tired of 'engagement' over community."

Discord was revolutionary once. It brought voice chat to gamers when nothing else worked. But then it grew. And grew. And became everything it promised to replace: a bloated, surveillance-capitalism machine that treats your communities as data mines.

Ripcord is our answer. A return to basics. Communication tools that serve the communicator, not the shareholder. We don't have a "growth team" optimizing for addiction. We have engineers optimizing for respect.

We believe:

  • 01. Your private conversations belong to you, not our training models
  • 02. Software should get faster with updates, not slower
  • 03. Communities should own their spaces, not rent them from Silicon Valley
  • 04. Privacy is a right, not a premium feature
  • 05. Communication doesn't need "AI" to be meaningful
